## Model Summary

- Unit-cell period: 500 nm.
- 3R-MoS2 proxy film thickness: 25 nm.
- Equilateral triangular through-hole side length: 374 nm.
- Substrate: SiO2 proxy.
- Physics: Electromagnetic Waves, Frequency Domain.
- Boundary setup: top/bottom periodic ports and x/y Floquet periodic conditions.
- Material proxies:
  - Air: epsilonr = 1.
  - SiO2: epsilonr = 2.1025.
  - 3R-MoS2: epsilonr = 17.64.

## Audit Fixes

- The triangular hole is now retained as an explicit air-filled prism and included in the air material selection.
- COMSOL's default `wee1` feature is retained as a material-controlled all-domain wave equation. Extra manually defined `wee_sio2` and `wee_mos2` wave-equation features are not used, avoiding overlapping wave-equation features while letting selected materials provide the domain-specific permittivity.
- MoS2-domain coupling operators are defined:
  - `aveop_mos2((ewfd.normE/1[V/m])^2)`
  - `aveop_mos2((ewfd.normE/1[V/m])^4)`
  - `maxop_mos2(ewfd.normE/1[V/m])`
- The paper uses MoS2-domain F2/F4 proxies instead of full-domain `max |E|^4`.

## Interpretation Limits

This is a pedagogical COMSOL scaffold, not a quantitative reproduction of a published device. The optical constants are simplified and wavelength independent. The F4 proxy is a linear-field MoS2-domain average, not a true nonlinear conversion efficiency. The spectrum shows multi-peak numerical sensitivity, so conclusions should be stated as trend analysis only.
